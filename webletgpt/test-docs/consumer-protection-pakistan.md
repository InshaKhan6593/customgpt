# Consumer Protection Laws — Pakistan

## The Punjab Consumer Protection Act, 2005

### Part I — Definitions

**Consumer**: Any person who buys goods or obtains services for a consideration which has been paid or promised. It does not include a person who obtains goods for resale or commercial purposes.

**Defect**: Any fault, imperfection, or shortcoming in the quality, quantity, potency, purity, or standard which is required to be maintained by or under any law for the time being in force.

**Deficiency**: Any fault, imperfection, shortcoming, or inadequacy in the quality, nature, and manner of performance which is required to be maintained by or under any law.

**Unfair Trade Practice**: A trade practice which, for the purpose of promoting sale, use, or supply of any goods or services, adopts unfair methods or deceptive practices. This includes:
- Making false representation about the standard or quality of goods
- Falsely representing that goods are new when they are reconditioned
- Making misleading advertisements
- Selling goods at prices higher than the MRP without informing buyers

### Part II — Consumer Rights

Every consumer has the following rights:

1. **Right to Safety**: Protection against goods and services that are hazardous to life and property.

2. **Right to Information**: Right to be informed about the quality, quantity, potency, purity, standard, and price of goods or services.

3. **Right to Choose**: Access to a variety of goods and services at competitive prices.

4. **Right to be Heard**: Consumer interests will receive due consideration at appropriate forums.

5. **Right to Seek Redressal**: Right to seek redressal against unfair trade practices, defective goods, or deficiency of services.

6. **Right to Consumer Education**: Right to acquire knowledge and skills necessary for informed consumer decision-making.

### Part III — Filing a Consumer Complaint

#### Who Can File?
- The consumer themselves
- Any recognized consumer association
- One or more consumers with the same interest
- The government (central or provincial)

#### Where to File?
Consumer complaints are filed based on the value of goods/services:
- **District Forum**: Claims up to PKR 1,000,000 (10 Lakh)
- **Provincial Commission**: Claims between PKR 1,000,000 and PKR 10,000,000
- **National Commission**: Claims exceeding PKR 10,000,000

#### Time Limit
A consumer complaint must be filed within TWO (2) YEARS from the date on which the cause of action arises. The forum may condone delay if the consumer shows sufficient cause.

#### What to Include
A complaint must contain:
- Name and address of the complainant and opposite party
- Description of goods purchased or services availed
- Date of purchase and amount paid
- Nature of the defect or deficiency
- Relief sought by the consumer

### Part IV — Remedies and Relief

Upon finding a complaint justified, the forum may order:

1. **Removal of defects**: Order the seller to remove the defect from goods
2. **Replacement**: Direct replacement of defective goods with new goods
3. **Refund**: Return of the price paid by the consumer
4. **Compensation**: Payment for loss or injury suffered due to negligence
5. **Discontinue unfair trade practice**: Prevent the seller from continuing deceptive practices
6. **Costs of complaint**: Reimbursement of complaint filing fees and legal expenses

### Part V — Penalties

#### For Non-Compliance
If a trader fails to comply with an order of the consumer forum:
- Imprisonment up to 3 years, OR
- Fine up to PKR 50,000, OR
- Both

#### For False Complaints
If a consumer files a frivolous or vexatious complaint:
- Fine up to PKR 10,000
- Compensation to the opposite party for any loss suffered

## E-Commerce Consumer Protection (2023 Amendment)

### Online Purchase Rights
Consumers making online purchases have additional protections:
- Right to a 7-day cooling-off period for returns
- Right to full refund if goods not delivered within 30 days
- Right to accurate product descriptions and images
- Protection against unauthorized charges
- Right to secure payment processing

### Digital Receipt Requirements
All online sellers must provide:
- Electronic receipt within 24 hours of purchase
- Order tracking information
- Clear return and refund policy
- Customer support contact information
