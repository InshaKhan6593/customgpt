# Pakistan Contract Law — Key Provisions

## Chapter 1: Formation of Contracts

### Section 2 — Definitions
A "contract" is an agreement enforceable by law. An agreement is every promise and every set of promises forming the consideration for each other. A "promise" is a proposal that has been accepted.

### Section 10 — What Agreements Are Contracts
All agreements are contracts if they are made by the free consent of parties competent to contract, for a lawful consideration and with a lawful object, and are not hereby expressly declared to be void.

### Section 11 — Who Are Competent to Contract
Every person is competent to contract who is of the age of majority according to the law to which he is subject, and who is of sound mind, and is not disqualified from contracting by any law to which he is subject.

### Section 14 — Free Consent
Consent is said to be free when it is not caused by coercion (Section 15), undue influence (Section 16), fraud (Section 17), misrepresentation (Section 18), or mistake (Sections 20 and 21).

## Chapter 2: Breach of Contract

### Section 73 — Compensation for Breach
When a contract has been broken, the party who suffers by such breach is entitled to receive, as compensation for loss or damage caused to him thereby, such compensation for loss or damage as naturally arose in the usual course of things from such breach, or which the parties knew, when they made the contract, to be likely to result from the breach of it.

Such compensation is not to be given for any remote and indirect loss or damage sustained by reason of the breach. The maximum compensation shall not exceed the amount specified in the contract, if any liquidated damages clause exists.

### Section 74 — Liquidated Damages and Penalty
When a contract has been broken, if a sum is named in the contract as the amount to be paid in case of such breach, or if the contract contains any other stipulation by way of penalty, the party complaining of the breach is entitled, whether or not actual damage or loss is proved to have been caused thereby, to receive from the party who has broken the contract reasonable compensation not exceeding the amount so named or, as the case may be, the penalty stipulated for.

### Section 75 — Compensation for Rescission
A person who rightfully rescinds a contract is entitled to compensation for any damage which he has sustained through the non-fulfilment of the contract.

## Chapter 3: Types of Breach

### Material Breach
A material breach occurs when one party fails to perform a substantial part of the contract, depriving the other party of the benefit they expected. In such cases, the non-breaching party may terminate the contract and seek damages.

Examples of material breach include:
- Complete failure to deliver goods or services
- Delivering goods that are fundamentally different from what was agreed
- Failure to pay the agreed price
- Unauthorized disclosure of confidential information

### Minor Breach (Partial Breach)
A minor breach occurs when a party fails to perform some obligation under the contract but does not deprive the other party of the essential benefit of the agreement. The non-breaching party cannot terminate the contract but may seek damages for the specific shortcoming.

Examples include:
- Late delivery by a few days when time is not of the essence
- Minor quality deviations within acceptable tolerances
- Administrative delays in documentation

## Chapter 4: Remedies Available

### 4.1 Compensatory Damages
These are monetary awards designed to put the injured party in the position they would have been in had the contract been properly performed. They include:
- **Direct damages**: Losses directly caused by the breach
- **Consequential damages**: Foreseeable losses that result as a consequence of the breach
- **Incidental damages**: Costs incurred in dealing with the breach

### 4.2 Specific Performance
Under the Specific Relief Act 1877, a court may order the breaching party to perform their obligations under the contract. This remedy is typically granted when:
- Monetary compensation would be inadequate
- The subject matter is unique (e.g., real estate, rare goods)
- The contract terms are clear and certain

### 4.3 Injunction
A court order restraining a party from doing something that would breach the contract. This is commonly used in:
- Non-compete agreements
- Confidentiality obligations
- Exclusive dealing arrangements

### 4.4 Rescission
This remedy allows the non-breaching party to cancel the contract and restore both parties to their pre-contractual position. Rescission is available when:
- There has been a material breach
- The contract was induced by fraud or misrepresentation
- Performance has become impossible

## Chapter 5: Limitation Period

### Filing Deadline
Under the Limitation Act 1908, a suit for compensation for breach of contract must be filed within THREE (3) YEARS from the date when the breach occurs. After this period, the claim becomes time-barred and cannot be enforced through courts.

### Exceptions
The limitation period may be extended in cases of:
- Fraud or concealment by the breaching party
- Acknowledgment of the debt or obligation in writing
- Part payment of the amount due
